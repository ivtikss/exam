Product information control

Robot scanner for price label control

Pilot stage

We are developing an autonomous mobile robot scanner for checking price labels, barcodes, QR codes, and related product information in retail and warehouse environments.

How it works →
  • Price labels, barcodes, and QR codes
  • Retail and warehouse environments
  • Project status: pilot stage

Challenge

Manual price label checks require regular rounds

Price labels, barcodes, QR codes, and other product information require regular comparison with actual shelf conditions. The project is aimed at automating recurring control rounds and preparing data for further review.

01

Manual rounds

Checking price labels and product information requires repeatable rounds and takes staff time.

02

Checking labels and codes

Price labels, barcodes, and QR codes need comparison with actual shelf conditions.

03

Control in different environments

Retail floors and warehouse environments require routes and rules for recording visual information.

System

Robot system: platform, navigation, and visual checks

The system under development combines a mobile platform, route-control software, and tools for preparing visual information.

A

Robotic platform

A wheeled base and modules intended to move along a route in retail and warehouse environments.

B

Navigation and route

A software layer for planning routes and the sequence of operations in a working environment.

C

Visual control

Collecting and preparing visual information about price labels, barcodes, and QR codes.
